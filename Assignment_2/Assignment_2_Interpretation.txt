The statistical analysis of the heart disease dataset revealed important patterns in the distribution and spread of multiple health-related features. 
Histograms and KDE plots showed that variables such as cholesterol, age, and maximum heart rate are not perfectly normally distributed and exhibit varying levels of skewness. 
Boxplots highlighted the presence of outliers in features like cholesterol and resting blood pressure, indicating unusual patient records that may influence predictive modeling. 
Group-based distribution comparisons demonstrated how factors such as chest pain type, cholesterol levels, and thalach vary across different age groups, 
helping identify behavioral and clinical trends associated with heart disease risk. 
Overall, the analysis provided a clear understanding of data distribution, variability, skewness, and potential anomalies, which are essential for effective preprocessing and future machine learning applications.
